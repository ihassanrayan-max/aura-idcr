- Phase sequence should be four-step and deterministic: nominal monitoring; LoOP/trip onset; decay-heat-removal decision window; stabilization-or-blackout progression.
- Trigger chain should be explicit and bounded: offsite power loss occurs; turbine/generator output collapses and normal heat sink is lost; reactor trips and decay-heat management becomes the main problem; DC margin drains while IC flow is absent or inadequate; pressure/SRV/containment markers escalate only if the recovery path is missed.
- Add the missing code alarm metadata and live wiring for `ALM_OFFSITE_POWER_LOSS`, `ALM_CONDENSER_HEAT_SINK_LOST`, `ALM_DC_BUS_LOW`, and `ALM_ISOLATION_CONDENSER_FLOW_LOW`, then reuse existing `ALM_TURBINE_OUTPUT_LOW`, `ALM_REACTOR_TRIP_ACTIVE`, `ALM_RPV_PRESSURE_HIGH`, `ALM_SRV_STUCK_OPEN`, and `ALM_CONTAINMENT_PRESSURE_HIGH`.
- Extend alarm clustering so Scenario B does not fall back to generic cluster names. The minimum new cluster semantics should be `electrical_disturbance`, `decay_heat_removal_unavailable`, and `pressure/consequence escalation`.
- Scenario B needs its own storyline hypotheses. The minimum set should be `hyp_loss_of_offsite_power`, `hyp_decay_heat_removal_gap`, `hyp_station_blackout_progression`, and `hyp_pressure_consequence_management`, with the intended progression moving from electrical loss to missing decay-heat removal to looming blackout, then consequence control if missed.
- The first-response lane should stay tight: confirm the LoOP/trip picture; establish IC flow in the bounded recovery band; watch pressure, containment, and DC margin; acknowledge alarms only after the dominant picture is clear.
- Validator relevance should stay narrow and rule-based. The new operator-facing control should be IC demand/alignment, not a broad new action set. Pass if IC demand is inside the scenario’s guided recovery band; soft-warning if it is outside band or off-lane; hard-prevent if the operator tries to reduce/zero IC demand after escalation markers or deep DC/pressure stress are active.
- Success should mean “stabilized toward safe decay-heat removal,” not “returned to normal power”: correct hypothesis stable, IC flow established, vessel pressure and containment brought back inside bounded recovery limits, and DC margin preserved above the low-alarm band.
- Failure should be deterministic and bounded: unresolved IC demand plus rising pressure/SRV/containment, or severe DC depletion, or timeout before the recovery path stabilizes. Keep low-level vessel inventory as a secondary consequence, not the main storyline.
- Directionally realistic vs intentionally simplified: LoOP, trip, loss of normal heat sink, battery-limited response window, and passive IC recovery are in scope; full SBO electrical architecture, real BWRX proprietary detail, and black-box AI are out of scope.
